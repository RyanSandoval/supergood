AI News Roundup — March 18, 2026 (Enterprise + Product)

Published March 18, 2026 — 5 min read

TL;DR: Anthropic now captures 73% of first-time enterprise AI spend, flipping the script on OpenAI's revenue dominance. NVIDIA GTC day two continued with partner network awards as the AI infrastructure buildout rolls on.

Top Stories

Anthropic Flips Enterprise Revenue — Anthropic now captures over 73% of spending among companies buying AI tools for the first time, per Ramp customer data — a sharp reversal from OpenAI's historic lead. Axios

Spreadsheets Are the Real AI Interface — Microsoft, Google, and Anthropic are racing to embed AI directly into Excel and Google Sheets; data suggests spreadsheets, not purpose-built AI apps, are where enterprise adoption is actually happening. PYMNTS

IBM on Smaller, Domain-Tuned Models — IBM predicts smaller reasoning models that are multimodal and easier to fine-tune for specific domains will define 2026 enterprise AI — less "one model to rule them all," more specialized stacks. IBM Think

NVIDIA GTC Day 2 — NVIDIA honored its 2026 Americas Partner Network award winners as GTC continues; enterprise and industrial AI partnerships dominating the agenda. NVIDIA Blog

Shipping & Platform

Geely + NVIDIA Partnership Expands — Chinese automaker Geely is deepening its NVIDIA partnership across physical AI, enterprise ops, and industrial AI — targeting full transition to an "AI organization." GlobeNewswire

Supermicro Adds RTX PRO Blackwell 4500 Servers — Supermicro expanding its AI server portfolio with Blackwell-generation hardware to meet accelerating enterprise compute demand. StockTitan

Anaconda + NVIDIA Open Model Integration — Anaconda's GPU-accelerated Python environments now span the full enterprise AI stack, including open models for agentic AI workflows. Radical Data Science

CometChat Raises $6.5M for AI Agent Platform — In-app communication platform CometChat secured $6.5M to expand its AI agent capabilities, targeting real-time AI-assisted user interactions. FloydCT

Policy & Governance

EU AI Act Compliance Clock — The EU AI Act's high-risk AI provisions continue marching toward enforcement deadlines; financial sector and healthcare teams should be deep in compliance mapping now. Wikipedia / EU AI Act

India's ₹125 Crore AI Contract — ONGC awarded a ₹125 crore contract to CIPL for AI-powered IT modernization across 47 locations — a signal that government-scale AI procurement is accelerating in emerging markets. Sarkaritel

One Take

The Anthropic/OpenAI enterprise revenue flip is the story of the week. 73% of first-time AI buyers choosing Anthropic — after the Pentagon standoff, after the federal contract losses — tells you that enterprise procurement teams are making independent trust judgments that don't track with government contract news. The spreadsheet angle is the practical corollary: buyers aren't adopting AI through new apps, they're adopting it through tools they already use daily. If you're thinking about where to integrate AI for your org, start where people already live — not where the demos look best.

Action item: If you haven't mapped which of your existing tools (Excel, Sheets, Slack, email) have AI capabilities already included in your license, do that this week. You're probably already paying for capabilities your team isn't using.
