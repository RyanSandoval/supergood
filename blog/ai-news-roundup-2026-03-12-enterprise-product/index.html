AI News Roundup — 2026-03-12 (Enterprise + Product)

Adobe's 18-year CEO exits as AI reshapes creative software, Meta doubles down with $135B in infrastructure spend, and the federal government takes its first real swing at the state AI regulation patchwork.

Published March 12, 2026 — 4 min read

TL;DR

Adobe posted record Q1 revenue but CEO Shantanu Narayen announced his departure after 18 years, signaling a leadership reset as AI reshapes creative software. Meanwhile, Meta's $135B AI infrastructure bet, a Commerce Dept. report targeting "onerous" state AI laws, and a $150M enterprise agent raise show the money and regulation racing to keep up with each other.

Top Stories

  • Adobe CEO Narayen to step down after 18-year run — Shantanu Narayen will resign once a successor is named, even as Adobe reported record Q1 revenue of $6.40B (+12% YoY) with AI-first ARR more than tripling. Stock dipped on a tepid forward outlook amid fears gen-AI disrupts traditional creative software. CNBC · Bloomberg
  • Meta guides $115–135B in 2026 capex, nearly doubling last year — The spending surge targets data centers, GPUs, and custom AI chips, raising investor worry about compressed margins and declining free cash flow (FCF fell 16% in 2025). Motley Fool
  • Commerce Dept. publishes evaluation of "onerous" state AI laws — The March 11 report is the first federal attempt to map the fragmented patchwork of state-level AI regulation, flagging laws that require models to "alter truthful outputs." It won't preempt state laws outright, but sets the stage for potential federal override. S&P Global · Ropes & Gray

Shipping & Platform

  • Wonderful raises $150M Series B at $2B valuation — The Israeli enterprise AI agent startup closed a round led by Insight Partners just four months after its $100M Series A, with backing from Index Ventures, IVP, and Bessemer. Now operating in 30+ markets. TechCrunch
  • ServiceNow expands AI into healthcare and regulated sectors — New partnerships with Aiva Health (voice-driven bedside AI), Cohesity, and Prismforce extend ServiceNow's "AI Control Tower" into mission-critical, compliance-heavy workflows. Yahoo Finance
  • Zalando forecasts 12–25% profit jump, credits AI productivity — Europe's largest online fashion retailer says AI is driving efficiency gains across operations, guiding adjusted EBIT to €660–740M and launching a €300M share buyback. Shares soared. Reuters
  • xAI's Grok Imagine flags another update — The image/video generation tool (now at 1B+ monthly generations) continues its rapid cadence; Elon flagged a new update today following the folders feature added March 4. Basenor
  • OpenAI rolls out "Skills" for ChatGPT Business & Enterprise — A new beta feature lets teams turn proven workflows into reusable, auto-triggering instructions with admin role controls — essentially codified SOPs inside ChatGPT. Releasebot

Policy & Governance

  • China moves to restrict Western AI tools at banks and state agencies — Reports indicate Chinese government directives will limit use of certain Western AI platforms at state-run institutions, adding another layer to the US-China tech decoupling. Ctrl+AI+Reg
  • NIST AI Risk Management Framework gets enterprise tooling — Veeam and Securiti AI published a joint implementation guide mapping the NIST AI RMF's four core functions to concrete enterprise capabilities, giving compliance teams an actionable playbook. The NAS Guy
  • Eightco secures $125M for AI + crypto expansion — The company locked in funding with backing from Bitmine, ARK Invest, and Payward, plus new board-level strategic support for its AI and blockchain platform push. TipRanks
One Take

Today's Adobe news is the headline, but the real story is the subtext: an 18-year CEO stepping down because the board sees AI as an existential inflection, not just a feature cycle. When your AI-first ARR triples and the stock still dips, the market is telling you the old playbook isn't enough. Meanwhile, Meta's $135B capex guidance and Wonderful's back-to-back mega-rounds confirm that enterprise AI infrastructure and agent platforms are where the gravity is pulling capital right now.

Action item for product leaders: If you're building on Adobe, ServiceNow, or any incumbent platform, pay close attention to their AI roadmaps over the next 90 days — leadership transitions and partnership expansions signal where integration opportunities (and risks) are shifting.
