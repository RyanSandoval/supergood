AI News Roundup — March 25, 2026 (Enterprise + Product)

Published March 25, 2026

OpenAI killed Sora and blew up its $1B Disney deal in a single day, signaling a sharp pivot away from video generation toward infrastructure and capital. Meanwhile, Claude got full computer-use capabilities and Arm shipped its first-ever silicon — the AI stack is consolidating fast.

Top Stories

  • OpenAI Shuts Down Sora, Kills Disney Deal — Six months after launch, OpenAI is closing the Sora app and API, ending its $1B Disney investment and content licensing agreement as GPU costs and Anthropic competition bite. Source
  • Altman Hands Off Safety Oversight — Sam Altman is relinquishing direct control of OpenAI's safety and security teams to focus on capital raises, data centers, and the company's next major model, codenamed "Spud." Source
  • Arm Ships First-Ever In-House Chip, Meta Is the Debut Customer — Arm's AGI CPU marks the company's first foray into production silicon after 35 years of pure IP licensing; up to 64 chips (8,700+ cores) fit in a single air-cooled rack. Source

Shipping & Platform

  • Claude Gets Full Computer Use + Dispatch — Anthropic launched computer-use capabilities in preview, letting Claude click, scroll, and navigate apps autonomously; paired with Dispatch, users can assign tasks from their phone. Source
  • Google Launches Lyria 3 Pro — Google's upgraded music generation model produces longer, more customizable tracks and is rolling out across Gemini and enterprise products. Source
  • Alibaba Launches Accio Work — A no-code, plug-and-play enterprise AI agent from Alibaba International aimed at giving global businesses an instant, pre-configured AI taskforce. Source
  • DeepBrain AI Ships B2B Video Agents — Interactive AI avatar agents for enterprise use cases like customer service and training, now generally available. Source

Policy & Governance

  • LiteLLM Supply Chain Attack Hits 97M Downloads — A malicious file in the popular AI routing library harvested SSH keys and cloud credentials on every Python startup — a wake-up call for anyone running AI stacks in production. Source
  • CFOs Expect AI to Cut Headcount in 2026 — A Wall Street Journal survey of chief financial officers finds administrative roles most exposed as enterprises accelerate AI-driven workforce reductions. Source

One Take

Today's news is really one story told three ways: the AI stack is collapsing vertically. OpenAI exits video to double down on foundation model infrastructure. Arm stops licensing and starts shipping silicon. Anthropic turns Claude into an OS-level agent. The mid-layer commoditizes fast — companies that bet on point solutions (Sora, standalone video apps) are getting squeezed between foundation models eating up and purpose-built hardware eating down.

Action item: If you're evaluating AI vendors right now, weight durability of moat over feature surface — the companies that own a layer (compute, model, distribution) will survive; single-feature wrappers won't.
