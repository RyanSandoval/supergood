What is an AI readiness assessment?

It's the step most teams skip on their way to a disappointing AI pilot. Instead of picking the flashiest use case in the room, a readiness assessment scores every candidate workflow against effort and actual impact, tells you honestly which ones aren't worth automating yet, and gives you a sequenced plan instead of a scattershot of half-finished projects. The output is a prioritized opportunity map with real ROI estimates — not a 40-slide deck that says "embrace AI."

How the full assessment works

Three steps. Nothing hidden.

Discover

We interview your team and inventory every candidate workflow — the manual ones, the "someone should really fix this" ones, and the ones on your AI wishlist.

Score

Each workflow gets scored on effort to build vs. time saved or revenue impact. We flag which ones are agent-shaped, which are plain-automation-shaped, and which should stay manual.

Roadmap

You get a written 30/60/90-day plan ranked by ROI, plus a live call to walk through it and answer "why this one first?" for every item.

Fit check

Who this is actually for.

Good fit: marketing or ops teams with at least a handful of recurring manual workflows, a stack that already includes Microsoft 365 or a comparable suite, and a leader who wants a prioritized, numbered plan instead of a vague mandate to "do something with AI." Also a good fit: teams that tried a pilot last year, watched it stall, and want an honest read on why before trying again. Not a good fit yet: teams with only one or two recurring workflows worth automating, no real data or systems access to audit, or a mandate that's actually about headcount reduction dressed up as innovation — an honest assessment will say so, and that's more useful than a sales pitch that tells you what you want to hear.
